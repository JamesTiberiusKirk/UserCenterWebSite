COMMON EMAIL TERMS


Address - An email address is a name for an electronic postbox that can receive and send email messages on a network. Email addresses are written in a standard format that includes the username, ‘@’ the at symbol, and the email provider’s domain, e.g. username@example.com


@ - This symbol is pronounced as ‘at’. It is used in all email addresses to separate the username and domain name.


Inbox - Contains all emails sent to an email address, excluding suspected spam emails which will be stored in the spam, or junk mail depending on your email provider, folder instead.


Compose - Where a new email will be written.


Drafts - Contains all emails which have been partly or fully written but not yet sent.


Outbox - Contains emails which are in the process of sending.


Sent Items - Contains all emails successfully sent.


Deleted Items / Trash - Contains all emails which have been deleted. Emails can be moved from here if they were accidently deleted.


Spam / Junk mail - The folder or section of an inbox where an email service provider sends emails that it considers to be spam or junk mail.


Spam - Common name for unwanted or unsolicited emails received. Most spam is sent in bulk to a large number of email addresses and advertises some sort of product.


To - Lists the email addresses of the primary recipients. This is visible to all other recipients of the email.


From - Contains the email address an email was sent from.


Subject - This should be a short summary of an email’s contents.


Bcc - Short for "blind carbon copy", this is a copy of an email message sent to a recipient whose email address does not appear (as a recipient) in the message.


Cc - Short for "carbon copy", is a copy of an email message sent to a recipient whose email address appears in the message's ‘Cc’ header field.


Attachment - A file, such as an image, a word processing document or an mp3 file etc, that is sent along with an email message. It is represented by a paperclip symbol.


Reply - This is how to respond to an email received. The original email will be included in the response.


Reply All - This is similar to ‘reply’ only it will respond to everyone the first email was sent to.


Forward - This is how to send an email received to another email address.


Opt-in & Opt-out - Opt-in refers to the process of giving consent to be added to an email list. Opt-out refers to the process of telling the sender of an email that you no longer wish to receive emails from them.


Unsubscribe - Email term for opting-out, or otherwise telling the sender of an email that you no longer wish to receive future emails from them.


Blacklist - A blacklist collects known sources of spam. Emails can then be filtered against a blacklist to remove spam from these sources.


Phishing - A fraudulent practice in which personal information is captured through an email designed to look like it has been sent from a trusted company or person. Typically, phishing scams involve an email alerting the user to a problem with their bank or another account.
