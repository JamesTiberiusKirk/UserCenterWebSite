Internet Safety

How to stay safe online

It can seem like an impossible task to protect yourself on the internet when you don't understand exactly how the internet works behind the scenes, but fear not, you don't have to be an internet security expert to stay safe online. We have compiled, here, a list of easy steps you can take to protect your login credentials and personal information online.


Step 1: First step you can take to stay safe online is to never give out any personal information or passwords to people who unexpectedly contact you online. Whether that be via email, a messaging platform like Facebook messenger, or any other means of communication online.


Just like you wouldn't tell a stranger in the street the pin code to your bank card, don't tell your passwords to strangers online.


Step 2: Arrange with your friends, family or colleagues in person that you will contact each other online and exchange emails or usernames so you can verify that you're speaking to them online. This is important because of how easily someone can impersonate someone you know online and use that to extract information from you that you might not want to share with a stranger.


Step 3: The most common way people's login credentials get stolen is via a "phishing" attack. This involves the perpetrator sending you an email pretending to be someones else, for example, pretending to be facebook and asking you to click on a link* that will take you to a couterfeit facebook page. If you attempt to log in, your login credentials will go to the hands of the perpetrator. To protect yourself from this kind of attack you need to be skeptical of emails that ask you to click a link and then enter your login credentials.


link* - a link takes you to a different website or a different page on a website you are on


Step 4: ...



If you have undertaken all measures for protecting yourself online mentioned in the steps above, your personal information is safe from hackers (people trying to steal your login credentials and accessing your personal information).


You might still be concerned that the websites you are accessing like facebook have access to your personal messages. You will be happy to know however that facebook isn't interested in personal information you might exchange in messages with other people.


The truth is that to a certain extent you have to just trust the websites you are using aren't up to anything illegal.


Although the media will speak about controversies surrounding the big names like facebook or google, your data is actually safer with the well know companies because they are under constant close watch from every angle. This means it's harder for them to get away with illegal activities
